📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the global video game industry using Data Warehousing and Online Analytical Processing (OLAP) techniques. By processing historical sales data, the project aims to identify market trends, platform dominance, and genre popularity to provide strategic insights for game publishers.

🛠 Tech Stack & Methodology
The project follows a standard Data Science and Business Intelligence workflow:

ETL Process: * Extract: Gathering data from Kaggle (Video Game Sales Dataset).

Transform: Cleaning missing values, normalizing publisher names, and formatting regional sales (NA, EU, JP, etc.).

Load: Storing processed data into a structured Data Warehouse.

Dimensional Modeling: Designing Star Schema with Fact tables (Sales) and Dimension tables (Time, Platform, Genre, Publisher).

OLAP Operations: Implementing Slice, Dice, Roll-up, and Drill-down to analyze sales across multiple dimensions.

Tools Used: Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn), SQL, and Power BI/Tableau for visualization.

📊 Key Analysis Dimensons
Global Sales Trend: Analyzing the evolution of the gaming market from the 1980s to the present.

Platform Comparison: Evaluating the success of consoles (PS4, XOne, PC, Wii) in different regions.

Genre Insights: Identifying which genres (Action, Sports, Shooter) generate the highest revenue.

Market Share: Analyzing the dominance of major publishers like Nintendo, Electronic Arts (EA), and Activision.

📈 Key Findings
Regional Preferences: North America (NA) and Europe (EU) show similar trends in Action/Shooter genres, while Japan (JP) has a distinct preference for Role-Playing games.

The Rise of Action Games: This genre has consistently dominated the market share over the last decade.

Strategic Recommendations: Insights for publishers like Ubisoft or Sony to pivot towards niche markets or strengthen their "Blockbuster" (AAA) strategies.
